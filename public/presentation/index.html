Research Foundation

Needs analysis across 3 countries

Before building anything, we surveyed three target groups to identify real gaps in communication training.

Police Academy Educators
What content is most effective in the classroom? What resources are missing? How can new materials integrate with existing curricula?
Internal communication perspective
Active Police Officers
How well did academy training prepare them? Which skills were not adequately covered? What situations are most challenging from a communication point of view?
Internal & external perspective
Public & Community
How do citizens experience police communication? What skills should officers focus on to improve public trust? Where does communication fail?
External communication perspective

Surveys + interviews across Czech Republic, Lithuania, Romania · Anonymous, GDPR-compliant · Results informed the 6-module curriculum

Methodology

Methodological guide for trainers

We created a comprehensive guide so that materials become real learning — not just activities to check off.

4 methodological pillars
Relevance & motivation
Start with a real problem, then add theory. If participants don't see the benefit, the activity is just a game.
Psychological safety
Not comfort — an agreement that you can try, fail, ask, and disagree without ridicule. Prerequisite for role-play.
Structure that frees
Not over-structured or too free. For skills: more steps. For complex topics: hold the goal, let the group find the way.
Experiential learning cycle
Experience → Reflection → Generalization → Transfer. Without reflection, only the experience remains.
3 instructor roles
Expert  — Short, clear inputs. Provides frameworks and concepts.
Facilitator  — Guides the group, maintains safe space, balances participation.
Designer  — Chooses method, format, and pace to make learning effective.
Training preparation
  • Define observable learning goals (knows / can do / chooses)
  • Select activities by purpose, not attractiveness
  • Sequence: easier → harder, safe → exposed
  • One activity in depth beats two half-hearted ones
  • Always end with: "What is usable on Monday morning?"

policecom.eu/hot-call

Hot Call — Card Simulation

  • 90 cards per language: Situation + Escalation + De-escalation
  • 3–6 players + facilitator (Head Honcho)
  • ~1 teaching hour per session
  • Online (projector) & printable PDF cards
How it works
  • 1. Draw SITUATION card → players respond
  • 2. Facilitator draws ESCALATION or DE-ESCALATION
  • 3. Players adapt → more cards can follow
  • 4. Group reflection on techniques used
